About Me

I am a geospatial researcher specializing in the application of advanced spatial analysis techniques to address complex environmental challenges. With expertise in climate science, energy transition policy, and natural capital valuation, I work at the intersection of data science and sustainability to develop innovative solutions for pressing global issues.

My research interests include:

  • Climate change vulnerability and adaptation mapping
  • Geospatial analysis for just energy transitions
  • Ecosystem services valuation and natural capital accounting
  • Remote sensing applications for environmental monitoring
  • Participatory GIS for community-based resource management

I hold a Master's degree in Geospatial Science from the University of Indonesia and have completed specialized training in climate modeling at the Earth Systems Research Laboratory. My work has been published in several peer-reviewed journals, and I regularly collaborate with international organizations focused on sustainable development.

Currently, I serve as a consultant for various environmental projects across Southeast Asia, working with governments, NGOs, and private sector entities to implement data-driven solutions for sustainability challenges. I am particularly passionate about ensuring that vulnerable communities have a voice in environmental decision-making through equitable access to spatial information and analysis.

When not working with geospatial data, I enjoy hiking, landscape photography, and volunteering with local conservation initiatives.
